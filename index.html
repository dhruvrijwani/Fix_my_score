Key Insights

  1. We spent ₹29,000 in October '25, out of which ₹15,000 was spent between 1st–14th October, during which the conversion tracking setup was broken.
    • As a result, we saw barely any conversions in this period, which increased the overall CAC for the month.
  2. Since Meta wasn't reporting conversions until 27th October, the campaigns were not being optimized for purchases.
    • Although purchases were happening (as seen on Google Analytics), they were not reported on Meta, which prevented the algorithm from optimizing effectively.
  3. After Meta started reporting purchases, the CAC dropped significantly to ₹106, compared to ₹431 when conversions were not being reported.
  4. With a CTR of over 2%, the creatives are performing exceptionally well and are above industry benchmarks.

Key Insights

  1. In terms of the overall funnel -
    • While we received 195 purchases overall, there were 139 payments failed. The CAC would have been much cheaper if they'd have converted.
    • The number of app uninstalls is significant. The is indicative of the app user experience. We'll need to understand the issues in the app and fix them which will further bring our costs down.

Next Steps

  1. We will start to scale on Meta gradually, considering the performance.
  2. We will test out new creatives so we have more of hero creatives. Right now, since the campaigns are only spending on 1, we don't have any backup performing creatives.
  3. On the app's tech bit, we will need to understand why there are so many payments failed and uninstalls. If we need to bring the overall costs down further, we will need to improve the overall user experience on the app.
  4. On google, while we're scaling down, we will also test out new copies to further improve the performance.
