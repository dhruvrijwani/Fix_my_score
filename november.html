Key Insights

  1. Instagram led in user acquisition with 3,022 first_open events, followed by Facebook (2,090) and Google (323), showing Meta platforms' dominance in driving app installations.
  2. Signup completion rates were strong across all channels: Instagram (75%), Facebook (72%), and Google (82%), indicating good onboarding experience.
  3. Instagram generated the most payment successes (337), significantly ahead of Facebook (263) and Google (11), contributing to 43% of all successful payments.
  4. App removal rates were concerning with 4,666 events (71% of first opens), suggesting potential issues with user retention or app experience that need investigation.
  5. Payment failure rates were relatively low at 4.4% overall (290 failures vs 789 successes), with Instagram showing the best payment success rate among channels.

Next Steps

  1. Scale the winning Meta campaign gradually, increasing budgets by 20–30% week-on-week while closely monitoring CAC to keep it within the ₹93–₹110 target range.
  2. Optimize Google UAC campaigns by launching new thematic ad variations focused on “Low CIBIL Score” and “Credit Card Rejections” to broaden reach and improve relevance.
  3. Address the high app removal rate (71% post–first open) by conducting user research to understand uninstall reasons and improving onboarding flow and early value delivery.
  4. Aggressively test new audiences across both Meta and Google as we scale, ensuring consistent discovery of high-performing segments.
  5. Launch retargeting campaigns on both platforms once sufficient audience size is available, focusing on users who installed the app but did not complete a purchase.
